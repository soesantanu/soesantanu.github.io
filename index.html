About Me

I am currently a PMRF and Joint PhD research scholar, with my home institute at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras (IITM) and host institute at the University of Melbourne (Uni. Melb.), working under the supervision of Prof. V. Vetrivel and Prof. Matthew K. Tam.

I work in the field of optimization and variational analysis, with a focus on first-order algorithms for structured convex and monotone problems. My research interests include primal-dual methods, adaptive stepsize strategies, and its applications in various fields such as Machine Learning, Statistical Model etc. I am particularly interested in developing efficient algorithms that exploit problem structure and theoretical properties to ensure fast and reliable convergence.

Research Philosophy

Bridging theoretical mathematics with practical computational implementations to solve optimization challenges.

Future Goals

Developing optimization frameworks for machine learning and computational science applications.
