About Me

My academic journey and research focus areas in mathematics

I am a Research Fellow in the Department of Mathematics at Stanford University, where I investigate the intersection of geometric analysis and theoretical physics. My current research focuses on developing new analytical techniques for studying nonlinear wave equations on curved spacetimes.

My work has implications for our understanding of general relativity and quantum field theory, particularly in the context of black hole stability and gravitational wave propagation. I'm passionate about discovering deep connections between abstract mathematical theories and physical phenomena.

My approach combines rigorous mathematical analysis with computational methods to solve complex problems that have remained open for decades. I believe that the most profound insights come from bridging different areas of mathematics.

Prior to joining Stanford, I completed my PhD at MIT under the supervision of Professor Michael Chen, where I developed new existence theorems for harmonic maps between Riemannian manifolds. My doctoral work received the Richard Courant Prize for outstanding dissertation in applied mathematics.

When I'm not immersed in research, I enjoy mentoring students, contributing to open-source mathematical software projects, and exploring the connections between mathematics and art.

Publications

Selected research papers and preprints

Stability Analysis of Wave Maps in Lorentzian Manifolds
Rossi, E., Chen, M., Williams, R.
We establish new energy estimates for wave maps from globally hyperbolic Lorentzian manifolds to Riemannian manifolds with non-positive sectional curvature, extending previous results to more general spacetime geometries. Our approach combines microlocal analysis with geometric flow techniques to overcome traditional barriers in the field.
Journal of Differential Geometry, Vol. 115, Issue 3, 2024
PDF Journal arXiv
Geometric Flow Methods for Harmonic Maps with Boundary Conditions
Rossi, E.
This paper develops a new geometric flow approach to study the existence and regularity of harmonic maps between Riemannian manifolds with boundary. We introduce a modified flow that preserves Neumann boundary conditions and prove long-time existence and convergence under curvature assumptions.
Communications in Mathematical Physics, Vol. 398, 2023
